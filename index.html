Vegetables - Vocabulary Review


Vegetables - Edible plants or parts of plants,
used as food.


Produce - Fresh fruits and
vegetables.


Market - A place where people buy and sell goods, often fresh
food.


Grocery Store - A store that sells food and other items needed for the
home.


Fresh - Recently picked or prepared; not stale or
spoiled.


Ripe - Fully grown and ready to
eat.


Spoiled - Food that is no longer good to eat;
rotten.


Bunch - A group of the same items tied or held together ➲
a bunch of carrots.


Bundle - Similar to a bunch, often used for leafy vegetables ➲
a bundle of spinach.


Loose - Not packaged or tied together; individual items ➲ loose
potatoes.


Price per pound/kilogram - The cost of produce measured by weight.


Sale - A special promotion where items are sold at a reduced price.


Discount - A reduction in the usual price.


Seasonal - Vegetables that are available during certain times of the year.


Kilogram - A metric unit of weight ➲ equivalent to about 2.2 pounds.


Pound - A unit of weight used to measure produce ➲ equivalent to about 0.45 kilograms.


Organic - Produced without the use of chemical fertilizers, pesticides, or other artificial agents.

Questions and Answers
